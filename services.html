Our Services

Gold, precious metals, and commodities handled with documented precision.

Four service lines, one integrated delivery capability, operating under the compliance architecture of International Finance Corporation, Miami.

Overview

The operating premise.

Clodia International FZE is the UAE trade and clearing subsidiary of International Finance Corporation. The operational model rests on a single premise: in cross-border trade of gold and commodities, the deal is made at the point of sourcing and unmade at the point of compliance. Our four service lines cover every stage of that arc, from origin documentation to destination delivery, under a unified audit file.

01 Gold Importation and Exportation

Clodia sources and imports gold into the UAE through a framework built to the standard of the UAE Ministry of Economy's Due Diligence Regulations (Ministerial Decree 68 of 2024) and the OECD five-step Due Diligence Guidance.

Sourcing and procurement

Gold is acquired from certified mines through suppliers verified against the group's KYC standard. Origin countries are tiered: Tier 1 for low-risk jurisdictions, Tier 2 for medium risk, Tier 3 for Conflict-Affected and High-Risk Areas. Tier 2 and Tier 3 cargoes require an independent OECD Step-5 assurance report issued within the preceding ninety days. Without it, the consignment does not move.

Commercial framework

Pricing follows the London Bullion Market Association benchmark less a fixed discount, calculated on 99.99% fine gold content at the day of the refinery assay. Gold below 99.99% purity is compensated on its actual fine gold content, established by a DMCC Good Delivery accredited refinery in Dubai. Standard consignment size is one hundred kilograms per month over a twelve-month frame, with scope to increase by agreement.

Form, packing, and marking

Delivery is in dore bars of ten to 12.5 kilograms, minimum 92% Au, cast at the mine site, vacuum-sealed, and packed into UN-approved tamper-evident bullion boxes. Each bar carries mine code, bar number, gross weight, and cast date. A QR code on each box links to the digital chain-of-custody file.

Trial delivery for new sellers

Counterparties without prior transaction history with the group enter through a trial protocol: less than fifteen kilograms of fine gold, cash and carry, full export documentation, payment in Dubai on confirmed assay. The trial is a compliance and operational test at reduced scale. Passing it opens the standard framework.

Standard framework for known sellers

The seller opens an account with IFB International Finance Bank, the group's banking channel. The buyer deposits the tranche purchase price into the seller's IFB account, where funds are blocked until the Dubai refinery issues the certified assay. Release is against the certified fine gold content, less the deductions set out in the Sales and Purchase Agreement. Settlement can be in cash in Dubai, in another currency by wire, or transferred to a third-party bank at the seller's written instruction.

Secure logistics

Transport from origin airport to Dubai uses Emirates SkyCargo Secure Vault service or equivalent, insured at 110% of LME value through Lloyd's, claims payable in Dubai. Advance Cargo Information filing is made with Dubai Customs twenty-four hours before each flight, in compliance with Customs Notice 7/2023. Clearance is handled by Clodia at Dubai International, and the cargo moves directly to the assigned refinery under armoured escort.

Regulatory file

Each shipment travels with the full documentary set: certificate of origin, certificate of ownership, commercial and customs invoices, conflict-free gold declaration, bill of lading, packing list, tax and customs paid receipts, certificate of assay, export permit, airway bill, insurance certificate, authorisation to sell to IFB Holdings LLC as the group's US buyer entity, UAE customs clearance, MoE DNFBP registration, MoEC Responsible-Sourcing Self-Assessment Questionnaire, refinery pro-forma approval, and the ACI filing number. Breach of the sanctions covenant constitutes an event of default.

02 Precious Metals Trading

Beyond gold, Clodia trades silver, platinum, and palladium for industrial, investment, and manufacturing counterparties.

Portfolio

Physical supply across the four precious metals, sourced to the same compliance standard applied to gold, with the tiered CAHRA framework and OECD alignment extended across the product set.

Market intelligence

Precious metals pricing responds to monetary policy, geopolitical events, industrial demand, and currency movement. We supply counterparties with commercially relevant intelligence, not market commentary.

Risk management

Hedging structures and financial instruments that protect positions against adverse price movement, structured in coordination with the group's financial infrastructure.

Ethical sourcing

Suppliers are required to demonstrate responsible mining practice at the point of origin. Conflict-associated metals are excluded at the supplier-qualification stage, not later in the chain.

03 Commodity Importation

Clodia imports industrial minerals, agricultural products, and energy resources into the UAE.

Network

Sourcing across multiple continents. Supplier qualification under the group's KYC standard, including counterparty screening against the same sanctions lists applied to metals.

Supply chain

Negotiation, procurement, sea and air freight, UAE warehousing, last-mile distribution. One commercial counterpart, one invoice, one accountable file.

Quality

Pre-shipment inspection and, where specification tolerance is narrow, independent third-party assay. Cargoes that fail specification are rejected at origin rather than received and disputed on arrival.

04 Export of Finished Goods

Clodia exports finished goods from the UAE to markets across Europe, Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

Categories

Luxury items, consumer goods, industrial products. Client sectors span manufacturing, retail, and wholesale.

Scope

Packaging, freight booking, export documentation, customs clearance at origin and destination, insurance, last-mile delivery. Bulk shipment and high-value consignment both handled.

Compliance

Certificates of origin, export licences, customs declarations, and destination-country regulatory filings prepared by the Clodia compliance desk. Shipments to sanctioned jurisdictions are refused at the contracting stage.

Why Clodia International FZE

The four foundations.

  • Institutional backbone. A subsidiary of International Finance Corporation, Miami. The parent provides corporate backing; the US sister entity IFB Holdings LLC acts as the group's buyer of record in gold transactions; the group bank, IFB International Finance Bank, provides the escrow channel; Clodia provides the UAE trade and clearing capability. Counterparties deal with one coherent group structure, not a chain of intermediaries.
  • Compliance depth. UAE Ministerial Decree 68 of 2024, OECD Due Diligence Guidance, DMCC Good Delivery rules, Dodd-Frank Section 1502 conflict minerals law, UN, EU, OFAC, and UAE sanctions screening. Tier 1, Tier 2, and Tier 3 risk classification applied at the supplier-qualification stage.
  • Delivery record. Sharjah Airport Free Zone location, partner refinery network, secure carrier relationships, Dubai customs infrastructure. Shipments arrive on the quoted date.
  • Documented process. QR-coded chain of custody, digital onboarding, electronic signatures valid under the UNCITRAL Model Law and the US ESIGN Act. The paperwork is lighter and the audit trail is heavier. Both by design.
